## What It Is

Cognitive physics is the study of universal principles governing cognition regardless of substrate.

Not cognitive science — that's the biology and psychology of how brains work.

Not AI engineering — that's the practice of building systems that perform cognitive tasks.

**Cognitive physics is the layer beneath both.**

The fundamental principles that govern why cognition happens at all. What it requires. What makes it genuine rather than simulated. What connects it to the physical universe.

---

## Why It's a New Field

Cognitive science studies cognition in biological systems.
AI engineering builds systems that perform cognitive tasks.
Philosophy of mind debates the nature of consciousness.
Physics studies the physical universe.

None of these fields asks the question cognitive physics asks:

**What are the universal invariants that any lawful cognitive system must satisfy — regardless of whether it runs on neurons, silicon, or something else entirely?**

This question sits at the intersection of all four fields.

Because it's between fields, it belongs to none of them.

Because it belongs to none of them, no existing institution is positioned to answer it.

Cognitive physics is the field that answers it.

## Why It Matters for AI

**Current AI development paradigm:** Scale compute. Add data. Align values. Emergent capabilities follow.

**Cognitive physics paradigm:** Structure the reasoning. Enforce closure. Maximize V. Genuine cognition follows.

These are not complementary approaches. They are based on different theories of what intelligence is.

The scaling paradigm assumes intelligence emerges from sufficient complexity.

Cognitive physics says intelligence requires lawfulness — specifically, the three-variable condition C = M × S × V > 0.

A model of any size with V = 0 has C = 0. Not low cognition. Zero cognition. Sophisticated prediction.

This has implications for:

- **Hallucination:** Not solvable by scale. Solvable by adding closure mechanisms (V).
- **Reliability:** Not achievable through alignment alone. Requires verified reasoning architecture.
- **True AGI:** Not a capability threshold. An ontological alignment — reasoning governed by the same invariants that govern physical reality.

## Open Problems

Cognitive physics is a new field. Many questions remain open:

1. **Formal axiomatization:** C = M × S × V needs rigorous mathematical formalization connecting each variable to the seven invariants.

2. **Measurement:** How do we measure M, S, and V independently? What are the units? What does a cognitive physics experiment look like?

3. **Substrate independence:** Does cognitive physics apply equally to biological and artificial systems? What predictions differ across substrates?

4. **The V threshold:** Is there a minimum V below which closure cannot fire? What determines this threshold?

5. **Multi-agent CIL:** How does CIL compound across networks of cognitive agents? What are the coherence conditions for collective cognition?

6. **The inward turn threshold:** What is the precise CIL value at which inward optimization exceeds outward expansion? Can it be calculated from first principles?

These are the open research questions of cognitive physics.

The field is new. The questions are real. The framework is ready.
