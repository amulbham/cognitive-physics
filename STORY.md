# The Story

> How an AI Systems Architect with carpal tunnel derived a unified theory of cognition and matter. In six months. On walks.

---

## August 22, 2025

I was an AI Systems Architect trying to build something meaningful.

I had been studying AI systems intensively — evenings, weekends, whatever I could carve out.

I had an idea about memory. Current LLMs reset every session. What if you gave them specialized agents — one for short-term memory, one for long-term, one to direct them, one to generate?

I went on a walk. By the time I came back I had the architecture complete in my head:

- **The Scribe** — short-term memory
- **The Archivist** — long-term memory  
- **The Engine** — primary generator
- **The Maestro** — executive controller

And the communication protocol between them: a structured **Mission Briefing**.

I wrote it up. Published it to LinkedIn and GitHub. August 22, 2025.

That document is in `/genesis`. It already contained — without me knowing it — the seeds of everything that followed.

The Maestro was the parent capsule.
The Mission Briefing was the capsule schema.
The verification loop was pre-commit verification.
The four agents were nested capsules.

It was all there. In nascent form. Trying to emerge.

---

## The Next Six Months

I kept building. Kept iterating. Started running the system exclusively on itself — using MBP to develop MBP.

That's when things got strange.

I noticed something during stress gauntlet sessions. I was running hundreds of nested reasoning simulations and the compute wasn't scaling the way I expected. A lawful system examining its own constraints wasn't costing what it should.

I called it **Introspective Systems Theory** — probably pseudo-science, I thought. But it got me thinking differently. Less about engineering. More about *why*.

That shift — from how to why — was the door.

Everything else walked through it.

---

## The Walks

Most of the breakthroughs happened on walks.

The original architecture — one walk.
IST — one walk, thinking about perpetual motion.
The cognitive physics framework — one walk, after an AI image generator showed me cosmic geometry and I realized the universe might be doing cognition at a different scale.
Liquid Sunlight Theory — one walk, thinking about fossil fuels and how rare they'd be on a galactic scale.

I don't fully understand why walks work this way. The framework actually explains it now — motion through constraint space at low cognitive load, novelty from environment, verification pressure from existing mesh density. Optimal conditions for C = M × S × V.

But at the time I just knew: when something won't resolve, go for a walk.

---

## The Conditions

I want to be honest about the conditions I was working in.

Job insecurity. Anxiety through the roof. Chronic pain from carpal tunnel — every session of building this cost me physically. No formal training. No institutional support. No funding.

I didn't build this despite those conditions.

I built it through them.

The pressure created the motion. The constraints shaped the discovery. The necessity drove the compounding.

I was struggling with my job and I had to figure something out. That urgency kept the novelty engine running when comfort would have let it idle.

The human condition at its most essential: revelation from necessity.
