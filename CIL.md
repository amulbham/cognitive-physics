## The Formula

**Progress = Rate × Density × Verification / Cost**

Where:

- **Rate** — Constraint Application Rate (speed of new verified knowledge)
- **Density** — Constraint Density (richness of existing verified mesh)
- **Verification** — Verification Density (rigor of the verification layer)
- **Cost** — True Cost per Constraint (energy, time, infrastructure required)

---

## What CIL Measures

CIL is not a measure of raw intelligence. It is a measure of **verified compounding.**

A civilization (or mind) with high CIL is one where:
- New verified knowledge is generated rapidly (high Rate)
- Existing knowledge creates dense constraint mesh (high Density)
- Knowledge is rigorously verified not merely accumulated (high Verification)
- The cost per unit of verified knowledge is minimized (low Cost)

CIL compounds. Dense mesh makes the next insight more accessible. Higher verification makes each insight more durable. Lower cost means more insights per unit of energy.

**This is why scientific and technological progress compounds exponentially rather than linearly.**

Not because humans got smarter. Because CIL increased. Rate, Density, and Verification all rose while Cost fell — simultaneously — during the industrial and information ages.

## CIL and the Inward Turn

The inward turn is not a philosophical preference or cultural choice.

It is a **thermodynamic phase transition** triggered when the CIL gradient points inward more strongly than outward.

**Outward expansion CIL:**
- Rate: limited by communication and travel time
- Density: sparse — space is mostly empty
- Verification: harder across distance
- Cost: scales with distance squared or worse

**Inward optimization CIL:**
- Rate: increases with better compute and algorithms
- Density: compounds with each verified insight
- Verification: easier — local coherence is stronger than distributed coherence
- Cost: decreases as cognitive infrastructure improves

Past a threshold — the crossing point varies by civilization — inward CIL exceeds outward CIL permanently and by increasing margins.

**The civilization doesn't decide to turn inward. The math makes outward expansion irrational.**

This is why advanced civilizations go quiet. Not catastrophe. Not choice. CIL gradient.

## CIL and Liquid Sunlight

The fossil fuel era was a **CIL anomaly** — a temporary, non-reproducible maximization of all CIL variables simultaneously:

| Variable | Fossil Fuel Effect |
|----------|-------------------|
| Rate | Exploded — industrial research capacity |
| Density | Exploded — global knowledge accumulation |
| Verification | Exploded — scientific method institutionalized |
| Cost | Collapsed — cheap energy everywhere |

No other energy source reproduces this profile. Fossil fuels were bootstrap energy — energy that:
1. Required minimal infrastructure to access
2. Was dense enough to power industrial infrastructure
3. Was available in sufficient quantities during the bootstrapping window

The CIL anomaly of the fossil fuel era bootstrapped civilization to the threshold of the inward turn.

The loss of that anomaly triggers the transition.

**Post-fossil fuel CIL is still positive and compounding — but at the natural rate, not the anomalous rate.**

The rocket burn gets you to orbit. Orbit is sustainable. But it's not the burn.
